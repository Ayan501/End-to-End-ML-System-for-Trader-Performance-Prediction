
INSIGHT 1 — Fear Days Are the Most Profitable

Extreme Fear days show the highest average PnL (+$500).
Greed days are the only sentiment with negative average PnL (-$86).
This is counterintuitive but consistent: when the market panics,
skilled traders capture mispricing and close positions profitably.

INSIGHT 2 — Greed Makes Traders Oversize Their Positions

On Greed days, average position size reaches $7,807 — the highest.
Yet PnL on those same days is the only negative bucket.
Traders are overconfident during bull sentiment and take on too much risk.
This is a classic behavioral bias: overconfidence inflates size, kills returns.

INSIGHT 3 — Traders Are More Active and More Bullish During Fear

Fear days average 4.52 trades per day vs 3.87 on Greed days.
Long ratio is also higher during Fear (41.6%) than Greed (37.6%).
This suggests traders are contrarian: they buy dips during fear
and become cautious or go short when everyone else is euphoric.

STRATEGY 1 — The Fear Accumulation Rule

When the Fear/Greed index is below 30 (Fear zone):
  - Increase trade frequency by up to 20%
  - Allow long exposure up to 60% of portfolio
  - Keep average position size below $5,000
Reason: this matches the behavior of the most profitable days.

STRATEGY 2 — The Greed Brake Rule

When the Fear/Greed index is above 60 (Greed zone):
  - Cap position size at 50% of your normal maximum
  - Reduce leverage, especially for large position traders
  - Prefer flat or short-biased positions
Reason: large positions on greed days are the biggest source of losses.
